Our Story

Seven Decades of
Inspiring Youth

Founded in 1955 by Captain Kanwar Harbhajan Singh, IIGS has been a beacon for Sikh youth across the world — nurturing faith, leadership, and community through over 100 camps worldwide.

A Legacy of Service

At the age of nineteen, Captain Kanwar Harbhajan Singh — known lovingly as "Papa Ji" — founded Young Sikh Missionaries in Lucknow, India. In 1972, he pioneered the co-ed Sikh Youth camping movement, and by 1979 the Institute went international when the 12th camp was held in Kathmandu, Nepal.

Moving to the U.S. in 1985, Papa Ji established IIGS headquarters in Southern California and began the iconic Darbar-E-Khalsa celebration in 1986. Today, IIGS continues his vision — reaching Sikh youth through camps, education, and community events worldwide.

Our Mission

To ensure the continuation of Sikhi through reaching out to Sikh youth around the world — rekindling the Sikh Spirit in every heart and bringing the pristine Khalsa Glory to the Khalsa Panth.

Our Vision

A world where every Sikh youth is connected to their faith, heritage, and community — empowered to lead with the values of Guru Gobind Singh Ji and serve humanity with compassion.

Annual Celebration

Darbar-E-Khalsa

Every December 25th, over 15,000 Sikhs unite at the LA Fairgrounds in Pomona, California for the grandest celebration of Guru Gobind Singh Ji's birthday — a tradition started by IIGS in 1986.

Asa Di Var & Kirtan

Melodious morning prayers and devotional music throughout the day

Guru Ka Langar

Sumptuous community meals served with love to all attendees

Khalsa Parade & Awards

Grand Khalsa Bazar, Kesh Darshan Parade, and youth award ceremonies
