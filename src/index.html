About IIGS

Capt. Kanwar Harbhajan Singh

"Papa Ji" — Founder

Born September 21, 1936, Papa Ji founded the Young Sikh Missionaries in Lucknow at just 19 years old. In 1972, the organization was renamed to the Institute of Gurmat Studies and pioneered co-educational Sikh youth camping.

By 1979, the 12th camp was held in Kathmandu, Nepal, taking the movement international. After moving to Southern California in 1985, Papa Ji launched the first Darbar-E-Khalsa on December 25, 1986 — drawing over 700 attendees in its inaugural year.

Over the decades, IIGS has organized 120+ camps across 4 continents and Darbar-E-Khalsa has grown to welcome more than 15,000 sangat annually.

Papa Ji passed away on January 30, 2011 in Mexico City. His legacy continues to inspire Sikh youth around the world.

Our Mission

To educate and inspire Sikh youth worldwide through Gurmat teachings, fostering a strong sense of Sikh identity, spiritual growth, and community leadership.

Our Vision

A global Sikh community where every young Sikh is connected to their heritage, empowered by Guru's wisdom, and committed to selfless service (sewa).

Our Values

Rooted in Gurbani — Naam Simran, Sewa, Equality, Courage, and Chardi Kala. We believe in learning by living, building community through shared experience.
